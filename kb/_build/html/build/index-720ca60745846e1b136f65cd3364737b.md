# Cortex Physiology

## What is this domain?

Cortical physiology examines how specific neural circuits in the cerebral cortex implement sensory, motor, and cognitive computations. Rather than studying networks in the abstract, this domain analyses the firing patterns, receptive fields, and circuit motifs of identified cell types in specific brain regions during behaviour. Findings from in vivo electrophysiology, calcium imaging, and optogenetics have revealed how cortical circuits encode space, recognise objects, plan actions, and store memories.

## Why it matters

Cortical circuits are the computational benchmarks against which neuromorphic systems are ultimately judged. Place cells and grid cells demonstrate that high-dimensional cognitive variables can be represented in low-dimensional population codes — an insight relevant to energy-efficient representational schemes in hardware. Visual cortex provides the biological inspiration for convolutional neural networks; understanding the actual circuit mechanisms highlights both the correspondence and the divergence between artificial and biological processing.

## Core concepts

- **Place cells and grid cells** — hippocampal place cells fire when an animal occupies a specific location; entorhinal grid cells fire at vertices of a triangular lattice spanning the environment, together forming a neural GPS system
- **Visual cortex organisation** — hierarchical processing from V1 (orientation and spatial frequency) through V2, V4, and the inferotemporal cortex (object identity); simple and complex cells in V1 as classical receptive field models
- **Mirror neurons** — cells in premotor and parietal cortex that fire both when performing an action and when observing another individual perform the same action; implicated in action understanding and imitation
- **Cortical columns and layers** — the laminar and columnar organisation of neocortex, with stereotyped inter-layer connectivity patterns (e.g. layer 4 → 2/3 → 5 → 6) repeated across areas and species
- **Sparse coding** — the principle that sensory stimuli are represented by the activation of a small fraction of available neurons, maximising information capacity and reducing metabolic cost
- **Predictive coding** — the hypothesis that cortex continuously generates top-down predictions and propagates only prediction errors upward, providing a unified framework for perception, attention, and learning
