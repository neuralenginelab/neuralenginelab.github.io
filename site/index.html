The Intellectual Stack

⬡

Foundations

The foundational ideas that established modern theories of learning, memory, neural representation, and intelligent computation.

⬡

Neuron Models

Mathematical descriptions of neural behaviour, from biophysical realism to computational abstraction.

⬡

Plasticity

The mechanisms through which neural systems adapt, learn, encode memory, and reorganize themselves over time.

⬡

Network Dynamics

Collective neural phenomena including oscillations, synchronisation, attractor states, and emergent computation.

⬡

Memristors

Theoretical foundations and practical implementations of memory-resistive devices inspired by biological synapses.

⬡

Neuromorphic Hardware

Specialized computing architectures designed to emulate the efficiency, adaptability, and parallelism of neural systems.

⬡

MEA Cultures

Experimental neural cultures and closed-loop systems used to observe learning and collective behaviour in living networks.

⬡

Connectomics

Mapping and analysing the structural organisation of biological nervous systems across scales.

⬡

Cortex Physiology

The architecture and functional principles of cortical circuits responsible for perception, memory, and cognition.

⬡

RRAM & Devices

Emerging electronic materials and memory technologies enabling adaptive and in-memory computation.
